An AI agent is a system that perceives its environment, reasons about what action to take, and acts to achieve a goal. Unlike a simple chatbot that only responds to a single prompt, an agent can plan multi-step tasks, call external tools, and adapt its behavior based on the results it observes. The core loop of most agents follows a perceive-reason-act cycle, often repeated until the goal is reached or a stopping condition is met.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation, or RAG, is a technique that grounds a large language model's answers in external knowledge instead of relying solely on what the model memorized during training. A RAG pipeline typically has three stages: ingestion, retrieval, and generation. During ingestion, documents are split into smaller chunks and converted into vector embeddings, then stored in a vector index. During retrieval, the user's query is embedded with the same model and compared against the stored vectors using a similarity metric such as cosine similarity, returning the most relevant chunks. During generation, those chunks are inserted into the prompt as context, and the language model produces an answer grounded in that context.

Embeddings are dense numerical vectors that capture the semantic meaning of text, so that pieces of text with similar meaning end up close together in vector space. Sentence-embedding models such as all-MiniLM-L6-v2 are popular for RAG because they are small, fast, and run locally without needing a paid API. Cosine similarity measures the angle between two vectors rather than their magnitude, which makes it a natural fit for comparing normalized embeddings.

Tool calling lets a language model request that a specific function be executed, with the model supplying the function name and arguments as structured JSON. The calling application executes the real function, then feeds the result back to the model as an observation. This pattern, sometimes called ReAct when combined with explicit reasoning steps, allows an LLM to look up live information, perform calculations, or take actions in the world that go beyond its training data.
